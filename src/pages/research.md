Drawing on ethnographic fieldwork I conducted in the United States and Italy, my dissertation asks how scientists today are developing new technologies for the restoration of artworks and cultural heritage. In particular, I am interested in how physicists, chemists, and biologists can bring their varied perspectives on &ldquo;nature&rdquo; to bear on what &ldquo;original&rdquo; or &ldquo;authentic&rdquo; art and heritage should look like.

### [TEDx Talk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b7dutcA7gb8)
I have had the pleasure of speaking at my high school, the North Carolina School of Science and Mathematics, in their TEDx conference. Drawing on the conference’s theme of “Growth,” I reminisce about my path from an aspiring teen neurobiologist who once poked the neurons of sea slugs to an anthropologist who studies the material practices of cultural memory.
